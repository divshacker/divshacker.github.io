About

I am interested in quantum error correction and fault-tolerant implementations of universal quantum computation using both discrete and continuous variable quantum systems. My research focuses on developing practical approaches to quantum error correction and implementing fault-tolerant quantum algorithms on near-term quantum devices.

Currently, I have completed my M.Sc. in Physics from Dayalbagh Educational Institute with a focus on fault-tolerant quantum computing. My work spans experimental evaluations on IBM Quantum hardware, quantum algorithm optimization, and contributions to the Qiskit quantum computing framework.

Research

Fault-Tolerant Implementation of the Deutsch-Jozsa Algorithm

MS Thesis • December 2024 • Supervisor: Dr. Shiroman Prakash, DEI Agra

Developed a fault-tolerant implementation of a quantum algorithm on trapped-ion quantum devices, addressing challenges in noise mitigation and computation reliability through advanced error-correction techniques. Incorporated the [[4, 2, 2]] quantum error-detecting code without ancillae and validated the approach using simulations, achieving a nearly 90% reduction in error rates across all tested scenarios.

Experimental Evaluation of QFT-Based and Conventional Adder Circuits

Research Project • January 2022 – April 2022 • Supervisor: Dr. Babita Jajodia, IIIT Guwahati

Conducted experimental evaluation of QFT-based quantum adder circuits on IBM quantum hardware, achieving a 92% accuracy rate on specific devices. Implemented optimized noise resilience strategies, reducing error rates by approximately 12% compared to baseline tests. Contributed to enhancing the understanding of quantum adder circuits and demonstrating their scalability for broader quantum algorithmic frameworks.

Two-Hadron Problem Optimization

Summer Research • May 2024 – June 2024 • Institute of Mathematical Sciences, Chennai

Optimized simulations for the Two-Hadron Problem using quantum-inspired methods, applying quantum algorithms to improve computational efficiency and accuracy. Designed quantum approaches to enhance simulation-based optimization, focusing on improved convergence rates and precision through the integration of quantum computing and classical optimization techniques.
