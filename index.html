About

I am a Physics Graduate Student with a deep interest in quantum computing, quantum error correction, and quantum information theory. My research focuses on understanding the fundamental principles of quantum mechanics and their applications in quantum computing systems.

I am currently exploring quantum error correction codes, particularly qutrit-based quantum systems, and their potential applications in fault-tolerant quantum computing. My work spans both theoretical foundations and practical implementations of quantum algorithms.

When I'm not immersed in quantum physics, I enjoy walking, fitness activities, and staying updated with the latest developments in technology and financial markets.

Research

Quantum Error Correction in Qutrit Systems

Investigating error correction codes for three-level quantum systems (qutrits) and their advantages over traditional qubit-based approaches. This work explores the potential for more efficient quantum error correction using higher-dimensional quantum states.

Lattice Quantum Error Correcting Codes

Research on lattice-based approaches to quantum error correction, focusing on developing efficient encoding and decoding algorithms for fault-tolerant quantum computation.

Classical Mechanics and Atomic Physics

Studying fundamental principles of classical mechanics, harmonic motion, electromagnetic theory, and atomic spectroscopy with applications to modern physics problems.
